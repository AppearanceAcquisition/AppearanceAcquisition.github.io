Multi-scale and multi-dimensional appearance features on cultural relics are important objective evidences for archaeology research. In view of the urgent demand in accurate and safe inspection and analysis on surface appearance of cultural relics, this project plans to develop a high-efficiency and high-precision instrument for appearance acquisition and modeling. Based on a differentiable computation model, we propose to map both physical acquisition and computational reconstruction into a unified framework for fully automatic, end-to-end optimization. We will research on illumination multiplexing with high-angular-resolution light sources and view/lighting consistent high-spatial-resolution modeling techniques, design large-scale, high-precision parallel light source and sensor arrays, and establish high-efficiency appearance computation and storage framework, towards the final goal of high-efficiency and high-precision instrument for acquiring and modeling surface appearance of cultural relics. The instrument will support, for the first time, high-accuracy, safe non-contact measurements of appearance features of cultural relics. The success of the research on the instrument has strategic importance, as it will advance the development of archaeology research, and push the research and development of high-end scientific instruments in our country.
