Multi-scale and multi-dimensional appearance features on cultural relics are importantobjective evidences for archaeology research. In view of the urgent demand in accurateand safe inspection and analysis on surface appearance of cultural relics, this projectplans to develop a high-efficiency and high-precision instrument for appearanceacquisition and modeling. Based on a differentiable computation model, we propose to mapboth physical acquisition and computational reconstruction into a unified framework forfully automatic, end-to-end optimization. We will research on illumination multiplexingwith high-angular-resolution light sources and view/lighting consistenthigh-spatial-resolution modeling techniques, design large-scale, high-precision parallellight source and sensor arrays, and establish high-efficiency appearance computation andstorage framework, towards the final goal of high-efficiency and high-precisioninstrument for acquiring and modeling surface appearance of cultural relics. Theinstrument will support, for the first time, high-accuracy, safe non-contact measurementsof appearance features of cultural relics. The success of the research on the instrumenthas strategic importance, as it will advance the development of archaeology research, andpush the research and development of high-end scientific instruments in our country.
